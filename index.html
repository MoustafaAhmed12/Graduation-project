2.1 - Research Gap

Research Gap

Despite the extensive literature on fair value accounting, a distinct research gap remains to be addressed.

🌍
Contextual Gap

Geographical & Market Focus

Previous studies have largely focused on developed markets, leaving a significant gap in understanding how Fair Value (FV) impacts Earnings Persistence specifically within the Egyptian banking sector under recent economic shifts.

🔍
Variable-Specific Gap

Persistence as Earnings Quality

While much research exists on Earnings Management or Value Relevance, there is a scarcity of literature that isolates and examines Persistence as a key dimension of earnings quality in relation to IFRS 13.

⏳
Temporal Gap

Recent Financial Transitions

Most existing Egyptian studies cover older periods. This study bridges the gap by including recent data up to 2024, capturing the impact of modern financial transitions and economic volatility on the stability of bank earnings.

Academic Context

Literature Review

Perspective Included Studies Core Conclusion
🟢 Positive Barth (1995), Muller (2011), Goh (2015), Penman (2007)
Enhances Relevance & Transparency:
Fair value accounting (FVA) provides timely, market-driven information that improves financial transparency, earnings persistence, and predictive ability.
🔴 Negative Okafor (2013), Laux & Leuz (2009)
Increases Volatility & Subjectivity:
Heavy reliance on FVA introduces artificial or pro-cyclical volatility and reduces earnings quality due to managerial subjectivity and earnings management risks.
🟡 Mixed Al-Khadash (2013), Long & Song (2009), Barlev (2003), Umar (2014), Oluwalola (2023)
Relevance vs. Reliability Trade-off:
FVA acts as a double-edged sword; it increases information relevance for investors but compromises financial reliability, with results depending on market liquidity and the specific hierarchy level used.
Research Problem

Problem Statement (1)

💡
Theoretical Debate: Prior research shows inconsistent & mixed results regarding the impact of Fair Value Accounting (FVA) on earnings persistence and quality.
🟢

Quality Improvement

Some studies suggest FVA improves the quality of reported earnings by reflecting real-time market conditions and reducing historical cost biases.

🔴

Estimation Risks

Others highlight estimation risks (e.g., Level 3 inputs) that may weaken earnings sustainability due to managerial subjectivity and valuation models.

Structural scale does not dictate accounting quality.

💡 Key Empirical Conclusion

"Operating performance matters much more than structural characteristics."

The ability to generate core operating margins (NI) and the relevance of reporting standards (IFRS 13) are the true determinants of bank earnings persistence, rather than simple bank size or historical profitability ratios.

Empirical evidence supports structural neutrality inside EGX banking systems.

Research Wrap-up

Conclusion

“
In conclusion, this study demonstrates that while standalone fair value accounting introduces market volatility, its interaction with net income (NI × FV) significantly improves earnings quality and persistence.

🔬 Empirical Takeaways

  • ✓ Earnings sustainability is driven by current operating profitability and efficient leverage management.
  • ✓ Rigid structural factors like bank size or traditional ROA metrics do not dictate earnings quality in the Egyptian context.
IFRS 13 & capital structure interact positively inside EGX banks.
